Session 1 · SEO & GEO06

The terms: GEO & AEO

GEO
Generative Engine Optimisation — getting your content surfaced inside AI-generated answers.
AEO
AI / Answer Engine Optimisation — a.k.a. AI Search Optimisation. Being the source an answer engine picks.
In practice the terms overlap — both describe optimising to be cited by AI, not just ranked by Google.

Session 1 · SEO & GEO09

SEO 101: E-E-A-T

Search engines don't just rank pages — they evaluate trust and usefulness. Google's quality framework:
E
Experience
First-hand, real-world knowledge — has the content been written by someone who has actually done this?
E
Expertise
Depth of knowledge — accurate, clear, well explained, beyond surface-level facts.
A
Authoritativeness
Credibility of the source — is the author recognised, with signals of legitimacy (brand, mentions, credentials)?
T
Trustworthiness
Can users safely rely on this — accurate, up to date, verifiable, non-deceptive?

Session 2 · AI Video Generation33

How to generate killer scripts

Find a trending video in your category (TikTok / IG / FB) → transcribe → deconstruct the structure → adapt it to your product with ChatGPT. Six beats, each doing one job:
Hook
Stops the scroll in the first 2 seconds — personal or provocative. "Okay, quick GRWM, I need to talk about this."
Relatable context
"This was me" framing the viewer recognises. "I used to think my skin was oily…"
Shift / insight
The moment of realisation that reframes it. "…turns out it was just dehydrated."
Product (soft)
Appears as a natural solution, not a pitch. "So I started using this lightweight serum instead."
Casual payoff
Why it worked, in real life. "Sinks in fast, my skin just feels balanced."
Light CTA
Conversational, non-pushy. "Just sharing in case you're dealing with the same."

How to use AI avatars on social

The play isn't "replace yourself" — real you for trust, avatar you for volume.
✅ What's good
✅  Scale the repetitive layer — localized & multilingual cuts (APAC / Mandarin), evergreen explainers.

✅  Keep your brand equity — a self-clone holds your face & voice, so it doesn't scream "AI presenter."

✅  Test ads at volume — UGC actors let you split-test many angles, cheaply.
✕ What to avoid
✕  A fully synthetic talking head on a feed — viewers smell it fast.

✕  A generic stock avatar for a personal brand — reads as AI-slop.

✕  Treating the avatar as your strategy — it's a shortcut, not a plan.

✕  Chasing skin-texture realism — lip-sync & pacing build trust.
Real you for trust-building. Avatar you for volume.

Mention the skill in plain English.

Best when you remember the name but not the exact trigger phrase.

Session 4 · Trends, Ethics & IP42

1 · Data privacy & sensitive information

Never upload
PII · customer lists or raw CRM data · financial account details · confidential contracts or legal docs · regulated data (health, finance, legal)
The seduction of "better outputs"
AI rewards you for more context, so the outputs feel better the more you share. That's the trap — most tools are not a private, trusted advisor.
Session 4 · Trends, Ethics & IP43

2 · Intellectual property of inputs

Uploading an image or prompt does not mean you own what the model produces.
Always check
Do you own the uploaded image rights? Who owns the output — and is commercial use allowed? Read the tool's terms of service.
Avoid
Mimicking real individuals (right-of-publicity risk) · training prompts on a competitor's proprietary assets.
Session 4 · Trends, Ethics & IP44

3 · Disclosure & trust

Audiences care about
Authenticity, transparency, honest representation — especially for influencer content, testimonials and UGC-style video.
The risk
Passing AI off as human · fake testimonials or "real people" who aren't. Disclose where appropriate; use AI as augmentation, not impersonation.
Session 4 · Trends, Ethics & IP45

4 · Integrity of output

AI is confident, fluent — and sometimes wrong.
The risk isn't only misinformation; it's credibility loss.
Common pitfalls
Publishing unverified claims · inventing statistics · overstating product capabilities · generic "AI tone".
Human review for
Tone · representation · cultural context. Test with diverse perspectives where possible.
Session 4 · Trends, Ethics & IP46

5 · Model bias & brand risk

AI models reflect biases in their training data — and can unintentionally stereotype, exclude or misrepresent audiences.
SME impact
Tone mismatches · cultural insensitivity · unintended offence. Especially risky for global brands, social-impact and inclusive messaging.
Human review for
Tone · representation · cultural context. Test content with diverse perspectives.
Session 4 · Trends, Ethics & IP47

6 · Over-automation & brand dilution

AI makes it easy to publish more, faster, everywhere — but volume ≠ brand.
What goes wrong
Inconsistent voice · generic messaging · "AI sameness" · audience fatigue.
So define
Your brand voice and what makes it unique · clear content boundaries.
