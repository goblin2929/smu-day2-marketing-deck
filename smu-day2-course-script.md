# PART ONE — AI-NATIVE MARKETING

### DIVIDER — AI-Native Marketing
**Say (the thesis):** "AI collapses the *cost* of producing marketing. The mistake is to read that as a cost-saving story. The real prize is **topline** — you can now do the volume of marketing that used to need a team, so you grow, not just save."

### [02] Before AI
**Say:** "Marketing used to be planning → campaigns → long cycles, and a human did every step — gather data, write, make the image, publish, analyse. The bottleneck was **people + time**, and people don't scale." Keep it quick; it's the setup for the next slide.

### [03] With AI
**Say:** "AI does the *doing* — processes more data, generates campaigns, publishes at scale, iterates non-stop. So the bottleneck moves to **human judgement**: creativity, judgement, clarity, direction. AI does the doing; you decide what's worth doing." This is the spine of the whole day.

### [04] Why does this matter for SMEs?
**Say:** "Faster testing → lower risk. More iteration → faster learning → find customers quicker. The punchline for a small business: **you can now compete on speed, not budget.**"

### [05] AI didn't change *what* good marketing is
**Say (let it land):** "It changed the **speed** at which you can do it. Everything from yesterday still applies — positioning, audience, offer. AI just makes the loop faster." Pause, then move to search.

# SESSION 1 — SEO & GEO (AEO)

### DIVIDER — Session 1: SEO & GEO
**Say:** "Search is splitting in two: ranking on Google (SEO), and being *referenced by AI* when it generates an answer (GEO/AEO). You need both now."

### [06] The terms: GEO & AEO
**Say:** "Two acronyms, one idea. **GEO** = Generative Engine Optimisation; **AEO** = Answer Engine Optimisation. In practice they overlap — both mean: optimise to be **cited by AI**, not just ranked by Google."

### [07] SEO isn't dying
**Say:** "First, kill the myth. *This* (point at the screenshot) is still SEO — the classic ten blue links, and they're not going anywhere. GEO doesn't replace SEO; it **sits on top** of it. Good SEO is what makes you *eligible* to be cited by AI." (Real Google SERP screenshot.)

### [08] SEO 101: Keywords
**Say:** "Three tiers — primary, secondary, long-tail — and the trade-off is **difficulty vs. winnability**. Real Ahrefs numbers: 'digital marketing' is KD 66, 90k searches — you won't win it. The long-tail 'digital marketing tips for small business' is KD 16, 140 searches — far less competition, clearer intent. **For SMEs, long-tail is where you actually rank.**"

### [09] SEO 101: E-E-A-T
**Say:** "Google doesn't just rank pages — it evaluates **trust and usefulness** via E-E-A-T: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness. Define each in one line."

### [10] E-E-A-T in a real article
**Say:** "Here's E-E-A-T in the wild — wisor.ai's freight-tools listicle. Experience = a named author with a real background. Expertise = a structured comparison table. Authoritativeness = it cites Freightos/Project44/Flexport. Trustworthiness = dated, 'reviewed by', compliance badges. **This is what 'proving it' looks like on a page.**"


### [12] SEO 101: Structure
**Say:** "Great content won't rank if engines can't **find, understand, trust** it. Three pillars: Crawlability (can they reach it), Technical hygiene (fast, mobile, HTTPS), Links (internal = what matters, external = endorsements)."

### [13] Your website traffic likely dropped this year
**Say (hook):** "Quick gut-check — whose website traffic is down this year? (hands) You're not alone. AI Overviews are absorbing the click that used to come to your site." Sets up the AEO case.

### [14] AEO / GEO evolution
**Say:** "In GEO, success is no longer **#1 on Google** — it's being **referenced by AI** when it answers. Traditional goals (rank, clicks, traffic) → GEO goals (be cited/summarised, influence the answer *before* a click, be a trusted source)."

### [15] The shift: mindset → operation → measurement
**Say:** "Three shifts at once. SEO mindset = win the ranking (keywords, page volume, traffic). GEO mindset = win the **citation** (concepts & clarity, insight density, influence & inclusion)."

### [16] A real Google AI Overview
**Say:** "This is the thing in action — a real AI Overview for 'best SME AI implementation consultant in Singapore'. The answer is **assembled and cited before any click**, and a handful of named businesses get all the visibility. The question is: are you one of them?"

### [17] Consumers are shifting outside Google too
**Say:** "It's not just Google. LLMs are becoming the **starting point** for search — AI search usage doubled year-over-year (Wix). So you're optimising for ChatGPT and Perplexity, not only Google."

### [18] Why AI SEO matters for your business
**Say:** "Fewer clicks from AI — but the clicks that come **convert 4.4× better** than regular search. They arrive pre-qualified; the visitor already trusts the answer that sent them. Fewer, but hotter."

### [19] Clicks drop, decisions don't
**Say:** "Even when the click doesn't happen, the **decision** often does — 80%+ find AI Overviews useful for finalising a purchase. AI summaries are **decision validation**, not just information. Being cited influences the sale even with zero traffic."

### [20] Classic SEO vs AEO
**Say:** "Same goal — get found — different playbook. Walk the table: KPI shifts from rank/traffic to answer-rate/assisted conversions; you have less *direct* control; you get cited across ChatGPT/Perplexity/Reddit/Wikipedia, not just the SERP; focus shifts to FAQ schema, entities, machine-readable context, and entity-building off-site."

### [21] Tools
**Say:** "Three buckets. **SEO** = Semrush. **GEO** = Profound, Peec AI, WorkDuo (do AI engines cite you?). **Website traffic** = Google Analytics — and here's the practical bit: to find your AI-referred sessions in GA4, go Reports → Acquisition → Traffic acquisition → filter Session source for chatgpt.com, perplexity.ai, gemini, copilot, claude.ai." (Logos are clickable.)

Both free — see where AI mentions you, and where it doesn't *yet*." Give them the URLs; have a few try it live.

# SESSION 2 — AI VIDEO GENERATION

### DIVIDER — Session 2
**Say:** "Where short-form video is heading, and — the real skill — how to **brief** an AI model so you actually get what you want."

### [22] Why video matters now
**Say:** "Preference for short-form keeps climbing, especially in SEA/Singapore. It out-engages static, and for an SME it's the cheapest way to showcase product and build a brand story when the ad budget is thin."

### [25] Using AI: pros & cons
**Say:** "Be honest about the trade. Pros: fast, cheap, no gear, easy to test. Cons: can look generic, may lack authenticity, copyright risk, and — the two that matter most — **no guarantee of a business outcome**, and AI is great at **mimicking/copying but weak on original creativity**."

### [29] Video → Video

### [30] How to generate killer scripts
**Say:** "Before the visuals, the words. Find a trending video in your category → transcribe → deconstruct → adapt with ChatGPT. Six beats, each doing one job — Hook, Relatable context, Shift, Soft product, Casual payoff, Light CTA. Read the GRWM example line per beat."

### [31] Writing a scroll-stopping hook
**Say:** "The first two seconds decide everything. A good hook is **specific, creates tension, speaks to one person**. Six types: question, contrarian claim, confession, POV, shock stat, curiosity gap. **Weak hooks describe; strong hooks provoke a reaction.**"

### [32] AI script example

### [33] How to generate a good video prompt
**Say:** "Now the visual brief — the **7-layer framework**: Object, Physics, Motion, Camera, Environment, Style, Negative constraints. Fill each box, then — the shortcut — **Ask AI**: drop your framework into ChatGPT and it writes the full prompt. Control each layer, or the model invents it for you."

### [34] One full prompt — all seven layers

### [35] How to use AI avatars on social

# CHANNELS & PAID MARKETING

### [38] Marketing channels overview
**Say:** "Where the AI-made content goes to work — email, organic text/image, organic video, paid ads, blog (SEO & GEO). Same content, many channels — AI lets one idea show up everywhere, fast."

### [39] Paid marketing
**Say:** "Two jobs. **Organic video** = discovery, top-of-funnel trust (TikTok/Reels/Shorts; hook in 2–3s; authenticity > polish). **Paid video** = distribution — scale what already works, retarget, test angles cheaply. AI's value is faster iteration; what doesn't change is targeting, budget discipline, message clarity."

### [40] Before you run ads: four questions
**Say:** "Don't spend until you can answer four: What's the goal? Who's the audience (cold/warm/hot)? What message are we testing? What does success look like? If you can't define success, you can't measure it."

### [41] Measurement
**Say:** "Organic metrics: retention (past 3–5s?), saves/shares, comments — views alone mislead. Paid metrics: CPC, CPL, CAC, ROAS. Know which number you're optimising before you boost anything."

### DIVIDER — Session 4
**Say:** "The fast part is easy. **This** is the part that protects your brand, your customers, and your reputation."

### [73] Six places AI marketing can go wrong
**Say:** "Six risk areas — we'll take each in one slide: data privacy, IP of inputs, disclosure/trust, integrity of output, model bias, over-automation."

### [74] 1 · Data privacy & sensitive information
**Say:** "**Never upload** PII, raw CRM, financial details, contracts, regulated data. The trap: AI rewards you for more context, so the output *feels* better the more you share — but most tools are **not** a private advisor."

### [75] 2 · Intellectual property of inputs
**Say:** "Uploading an image or prompt **does not** mean you own the output. Always check: do you own the input rights? Who owns the output, and is commercial use allowed? Avoid mimicking real people or training on a competitor's assets."

### [76] 3 · Disclosure & trust
**Say:** "Audiences care about authenticity — especially for testimonials and UGC. The risk: passing AI off as human, fake 'real people'. Disclose where appropriate; use AI as **augmentation, not impersonation.**"

### [77] 4 · Integrity of output
**Say:** "AI is confident, fluent — and **sometimes wrong**. The risk isn't only misinformation, it's **credibility loss**. Pitfalls: unverified claims, invented stats, overstated capabilities, generic 'AI tone'. Human-review tone, representation, cultural context."

### [78] 5 · Model bias & brand risk
**Say:** "Models reflect training-data bias — can stereotype, exclude, misrepresent. SME impact: tone mismatches, cultural insensitivity, unintended offence. Riskiest for global, social-impact, inclusive messaging. Review with diverse perspectives."

### [79] 6 · Over-automation & brand dilution
**Say:** "AI makes it easy to publish more, faster, everywhere — but **volume ≠ brand**. What goes wrong: inconsistent voice, generic messaging, 'AI sameness', audience fatigue. So define your brand voice and content boundaries up front."

### [80] Forward-looking trends
**Say:** "Three to watch: (1) search shifts to answers — more influence without traffic; (2) **iteration is the edge** — winners test faster, not spend more; (3) consultants become **filters** — judgement beats tool-choice."

### [81] What changes, what doesn't
**Say (the close of content):** "What AI changes — speed, cost, scale. What it does **not** change — accountability, trust, ethics. **Business fundamentals still decide who wins.** That's the whole day in one line."
